Illustrative deployment scenarios

Representative examples of how a Nexus rollout typically plays out across different city sizes and priorities.

Traffic layer Austin, USA

Cutting average incident response by a third

Austin's mobility team started with a single-district traffic pilot, feeding 40 intersections into Nexus's congestion map and adaptive signal timing. Within two quarters, response time to reported incidents dropped noticeably, and the pilot expanded to the full downtown grid.

33%Faster incident response
40→310Intersections connected

Energy layer Rotterdam, Netherlands

Spotting substation load anomalies before they became outages

Rotterdam connected its distribution network monitors to Nexus's energy layer to get ahead of aging substation infrastructure. Predictive load forecasting flagged three at-risk substations months before their previous inspection cycle would have caught them.

3Outages prevented (est.)
94%Peak grid capacity visibility
12 moEarlier fault detection
Public safety layer Busan, South Korea

One dashboard the operations team actually opens every morning

Busan's infrastructure team had incident data spread across five separate systems. Consolidating alerts into a single Nexus console — with full audit history per alert — cut the time staff spent just locating information before a shift even started.

5→1Systems consolidated
~20 minSaved per shift handover
100%Alerts with audit trail
Full rollout Lahore, Pakistan

A full four-layer rollout, staged district by district

Lahore's pilot began with the water utility's leak-detection sensors, then expanded to traffic and energy over the following year. Predictive maintenance flags across the water network alone are projected to offset the platform's cost within twelve months.
